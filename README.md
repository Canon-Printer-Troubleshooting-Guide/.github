<h1>Canon Printer Error Codes — Complete Reference Guide</h1>

<p>Every Canon Pixma printer communicates problems through error codes.
Knowing what each code actually means — and what genuinely fixes it —
saves time, prevents unnecessary part replacements, and keeps your
printer running long term. This reference covers every major Canon
error code category with real fixes, not just "turn it off and on
again" advice.</p>

<p>This guide is written for Canon Pixma printers used in the United
States and Canada, where regional driver versions and cartridge
models differ from other markets.</p>

<hr>

<h2>Paper and Feed Errors</h2>

<h3>E02 / Error 1000 — No Paper Loaded</h3>

<p>The printer sent a feed command and found no paper in the active
tray. This is the simplest error to resolve but it comes back
repeatedly for people who load paper without adjusting the guides.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Load paper into the rear tray or front cassette.
Adjust the paper guides so they sit snugly against both edges of the
paper without squeezing it. A loose guide causes the paper to skew
during feeding, which triggers the same error even with paper loaded.
Press OK or Resume on the printer panel after loading.
</div>

<h3>E03 / Error 1300 — Paper Jam</h3>

<p>Paper is obstructing the feed path somewhere inside the printer.
The critical thing most people miss: a torn fragment left behind after
clearing the jam causes the exact same error to reappear immediately.
The printer doesn't need a full sheet jammed — a corner the size of
a postage stamp is enough.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Open every access panel — front cover, rear
access door if your model has one, output tray area. Use a flashlight
to inspect the entire paper path. Pull any jammed paper slowly and
steadily in the direction of the feed path without tearing it. If it
tears, find every fragment before closing anything. Press OK after
clearing.
</div>

<h3>Error 1002 / 1007 — Multi-Feed and Transport Jams</h3>

<p>Error 1002 indicates paper jammed in the rear transport path.
Error 1007 means multiple sheets were fed simultaneously. Both are
more common with paper that's been sitting in a humid environment
or when the paper stack is loaded too full.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> For 1002, open the rear access door and remove
the obstructing paper. For 1007, remove all paper from the tray, fan
the stack to separate the sheets, and reload with a smaller stack —
no more than 60% of the tray's maximum capacity.
</div>

<hr>

<h2>Ink Cartridge Errors</h2>

<h3>E04 / E05 — Wrong or Unrecognized Cartridge</h3>

<p>E04 means no cartridge is detected in a slot. E05 means a
cartridge is present but the printer cannot authenticate it. Both
codes fire when cartridge contacts are dirty, when the cartridge
isn't properly seated, or when an incompatible cartridge is
installed.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Open the cartridge bay and remove each
cartridge. Press each one back in firmly until you hear and feel a
definite click. If the error persists, clean the gold electrical
contacts on the cartridge underside with a dry lint-free cloth.
Do not use alcohol or water on the contacts — dry only. Reinstall
and test. If using a third-party or refilled cartridge, an
incompatible chip may be the root cause.
</div>

<h3>E07 / Error 1660 — Cartridge Seated Incorrectly</h3>

<p>This error is specific to the cartridge position and lock state.
The cartridge is in the slot but hasn't clicked fully into the
locked position. It looks inserted but isn't making proper electrical
contact.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Open the cartridge bay, remove the cartridge
entirely, and reinsert it pressing down firmly and evenly from both
sides simultaneously. The click needs to be definitive — a soft
partial click is not enough to clear this error.
</div>

<h3>E13 / E16 / Error 1641 / 1686 / 1688 — Ink Depleted</h3>

<p>One or more cartridges has run out of ink. E13 and 1641 are
the standard depletion codes. E16, 1686, and 1688 appear on some
models when the printer is requesting confirmation to stop rather
than automatically halting.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Replace the empty cartridge promptly. Canon
printers use ink to cool the print head's heating elements during
operation. Continuing to print on a fully empty cartridge causes
thermal damage to the print head over repeated sessions — this is
not recoverable damage and the print head is not cheap to replace.
</div>

<h3>Error 1680 / 1681 — Incompatible Cartridge Combination</h3>

<p>The installed cartridges are not compatible with each other,
with this printer model, or with the regional market configuration
of the printer. This error is more common than people expect
because Canon uses different cartridge part numbers for printers
sold in different regions, and the numbers look nearly identical.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Verify both cartridges match the exact part
numbers specified in your printer's manual. A cartridge purchased
outside the US or Canada will be flagged as incompatible on a
North American model printer even if it physically fits in the slot.
</div>

<h3>Error 1682 / 1684 / 1689 — Cartridge Authentication Failed</h3>

<p>The printer detects a cartridge but cannot read its
authentication chip. This is distinct from a compatibility error —
the chip is present but unreadable. Common causes are ink
contamination on the chip contacts, a damaged chip from rough
handling, or a counterfeit cartridge with a non-functional chip.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Remove the cartridge. Clean the gold contacts
on the underside using a dry lint-free cloth. Let it sit for two
minutes. Reinstall and test. If the error persists across multiple
reinstallations, the cartridge chip is damaged and the cartridge
needs to be replaced.
</div>

<hr>

<h2>Ink Absorber Errors</h2>

<h3>P07 / Error 5B00 / 1700 / 1701 / 6830 — Ink Absorber Full</h3>

<p>This is the most misunderstood Canon error. The ink absorber is
an internal sponge pad that collects overflow ink produced during
automatic cleaning cycles. Canon tracks absorber usage through a
software counter — not a physical level sensor. The error fires
when the counter hits a preset threshold regardless of whether the
pad is physically full or not.</p>

<div class="warning-box">
<strong>Important:</strong> Resetting the counter without checking
the physical pad, or replacing the pad without resetting the counter,
both result in the error returning. Both actions are required for a
permanent fix.
</div>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix — Counter reset:</strong> Download Canon's Service Tool
for your printer model. Connect the printer via USB. Navigate to the
Main tab. Click Clear on the ink absorber counter. Power cycle the
printer after resetting.<br><br>
<strong>Fix — Physical pad:</strong> Replacement absorber pads are
available for most Pixma models and are inexpensive. Replace when
the counter is reset to ensure the error doesn't return prematurely.
</div>

<h3>Error 1710 / 1711 — Ink Absorber Warning</h3>

<p>The absorber counter is approaching its limit but hasn't
triggered the full P07 error yet. This is your window to plan the
reset before being forced to stop printing mid-job.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Perform the counter reset and physical pad
inspection described above before the P07 error activates. Continuing
to print through this warning burns through the remaining counter
headroom quickly.
</div>

<hr>

<h2>Print Head Errors</h2>

<h3>Error 1401 / 1403 — Print Head Not Detected</h3>

<p>The printer cannot establish communication with the print head.
This error is almost always caused by dirty electrical contacts
between the print head and the carriage rather than a failed print
head. The contacts accumulate ink residue over time and eventually
interrupt the connection.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong>
<ol>
<li>Power off and unplug the printer</li>
<li>Open the cartridge bay — remove all cartridges</li>
<li>Locate the print head locking lever (usually orange or grey)
and unlock it</li>
<li>Lift the print head straight out of the carriage</li>
<li>Dampen a lint-free cloth lightly with distilled water</li>
<li>Wipe the gold contacts on the print head underside in one
direction</li>
<li>Wipe the carriage contacts inside the printer the same way</li>
<li>Allow both to dry completely — minimum 5 minutes</li>
<li>Reinstall the print head, lock the lever, reinstall cartridges</li>
<li>Power on and allow full initialization</li>
</ol>
</div>

<h3>E31 — Print Head Error (E-Series Models)</h3>

<p>E31 is the E-series equivalent of error 1403. Same root cause —
dirty or poorly connected print head contacts. The fix process is
identical to the 1403 procedure above.</p>

<h3>Error 5200 — Print Head Overheating</h3>

<p>The thermal sensor detected dangerous heat levels in the print
head. Canon print heads use ink as a coolant — the ink flowing
through the nozzles dissipates heat from the firing elements. When
a cartridge runs completely dry and the printer continues operating,
the nozzles fire without coolant and the temperature spikes.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Power off immediately. Unplug the printer and
leave it for at least 20 minutes. Inspect cartridges and replace
any that are empty before restarting. If 5200 returns after the
printer has cooled and cartridges are full, the print head may have
sustained thermal damage from previous overheat episodes.
</div>

<h3>Error 5100 — Carriage Movement Error</h3>

<p>The print head carriage cannot complete its movement cycle. The
carriage motor detected unexpected resistance or lost track of
carriage position.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Power off and unplug. Open the cartridge access
cover. Inspect the carriage rail for obstructions — paper fragments,
debris, or a dislodged cartridge. With the printer unplugged, slide
the carriage manually left and right to feel for resistance points.
Also check the encoder strip — the thin transparent strip running
horizontally across the carriage path. Ink smudging on this strip
causes the printer to lose position tracking. Clean with a dry
lint-free cloth if smudged. Plug in and power on.
</div>

<h2>WiFi and Connectivity Errors</h2>

<h3>Error 2001 / 2002 — WiFi Setup Failed</h3>

<p>The printer attempted to connect to a wireless network during
setup and failed. The two most common causes are a 5GHz network
selection (Canon Pixma printers support 2.4GHz only) and WPA3
router security (not supported by most Canon Pixma models released
before 2022).</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Confirm your network is 2.4GHz before
entering the password. If your router uses WPA3 exclusively,
temporarily switch to WPA2/WPA3 mixed mode in your router settings
during the printer setup process. Also verify the password is
entered exactly — the on-screen keyboard on Canon printers is
easy to mistype on.
</div>

<p>For a complete WiFi troubleshooting walkthrough covering all
router compatibility scenarios for US and Canada users, the
<a href="https://printersolved.com/ij-start-canon-setup/">ij start
canon setup</a> guide covers the full WiFi configuration process
including what to do when standard setup fails repeatedly.</p>

<h3>Error 2700 / 2802 — Network Connection Lost</h3>

<p>The printer connected to WiFi successfully during setup but
subsequently lost the connection. This is almost always caused by
the router assigning a different IP address to the printer after
restarting — the driver is pointing at the old address and
can't find the printer at the new one.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Log into your router's admin panel (usually
at 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1). Find your Canon printer in the
connected devices list using its MAC address. Assign it a static
IP through your router's DHCP reservation settings. This locks
the printer to one address permanently. The printer's MAC address
is printed on its network status page — hold Stop/Reset for
5 seconds to print it.
</div>

<h2>Scanner Errors</h2>

<h3>Error 2111 / 2113 — Wireless Scan Failed</h3>

<p>The scanner cannot communicate with the host computer. This
almost always means the scan driver wasn't included in the
installation — the basic Canon driver covers printing only.
The scan function requires the full driver package including
IJ Scan Utility.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Fix:</strong> Uninstall all Canon software through Programs
and Features. Restart the computer. Reinstall using the full driver
and software package — not the basic driver option. After
installation, confirm IJ Scan Utility appears in your program list
and shows your printer as the active scanner.
</div>

<h2>General Reset for Persistent Errors</h2>

<p>When an error persists through standard fixes, a factory reset
clears stored error states that survive power cycling. Use this
as a last step before concluding the issue is hardware rather
than software.</p>

<div class="fix-box">
<strong>Factory reset procedure (most Canon Pixma models):</strong>
<ol>
<li>Make sure the printer is powered on</li>
<li>Press and hold Stop/Reset</li>
<li>While holding, press and hold Power</li>
<li>Hold both buttons for 5 seconds</li>
<li>Release Stop/Reset while keeping Power held</li>
<li>Press Stop/Reset twice</li>
<li>Press Power to confirm — the printer resets and restarts</li>
</ol>
After a factory reset, WiFi reconnection and driver reinstallation
are required before the printer will work.
</div>

<p>After any factory reset the driver on your computer needs to be
reinstalled to recognize the printer as a new device.
<a href="https://printersolved.com/ij-start-canon-setup/">PrinterSolved</a>
covers the complete driver reinstall process for US and Canada users
including the port configuration step that prevents the offline
error from returning after the first router restart.</p>
