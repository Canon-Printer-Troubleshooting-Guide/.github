<h2>Why Your Canon Printer Stopped Working</h2>


<p>When you first connect a Canon printer Windows 

searches its driver database and installs a basic 

generic driver. This driver disables wireless printing 

scanning copying and the Canon IJ Printer Assistant 

Tool without any warning. Everything looks fine until 

you try to use these features.</p>

<h2>How to Fix Canon Printer WiFi Connection</h2>


<p>Step 1 — Make sure your Canon printer is 

connecting to 2.4GHz WiFi not 5GHz. Canon printers 

do not support 5GHz and will not connect regardless 

of signal strength.</p>


<p>Step 2 — On the printer panel navigate to 

Settings then LAN Settings then Wireless LAN Setup. 

Select your 2.4GHz network and enter your password.</p>


<p>Step 3 — On your computer uninstall all existing 

Canon software through Control Panel then reinstall 

the complete driver package. This restores wireless 

printing scanning and all maintenance features.</p>


<p>Step 4 — Log into your router admin panel and 

create a DHCP reservation for your Canon printer 

using its MAC address. This permanently fixes the 

offline problem after router restarts.</p>

<h2>Pro Tips for Long Term Canon Printer Performance</h2>


<ul>

<li>Leave printer powered on in standby — 

prevents nozzle clogging from drying ink</li>

<li>Print one page weekly during idle periods</li>

<li>Update firmware through Canon IJ Printer 

Assistant Tool regularly</li>

<li>Assign static IP in router DHCP settings 

to prevent offline problem permanently</li>

<li>Use genuine Canon cartridges for best 

quality and reliable recognition</li>

</ul>
